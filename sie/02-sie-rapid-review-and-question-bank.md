# SIE — Rapid Review & Practice Question Bank

> Companion to the [in-depth study guide](01-sie-study-guide.md). Use the guide to *learn*, this
> file to *drill*. The question bank mirrors the real exam's style: one best answer, plausible
> distractors, and an explanation that teaches the underlying principle so a missed question
> becomes a learned one.

**How to use it:** Work a block, score yourself, and for every miss, read the *why* and find the
matching section in the [main guide](01-sie-study-guide.md). Re-take until you're ≥85%.

## Rapid review — the night before

**Markets & roles**
- Primary = issuer gets the money. Secondary = investor-to-investor.
- **Agent/broker = commission** (acts for client). **Principal/dealer = markup** (own inventory).
- Customer **buys at the ask, sells at the bid**; spread = dealer's pay.

**The Fed**
- Loosen: **buy** securities, lower discount rate / reserve req → rates down → bond prices up.
- Tighten: **sell** securities → rates up → bond prices down.
- Open-market operations = most-used tool.

**Equity**
- Common: vote, dividends (only if declared), residual (paid **last**), preemptive right.
- Cumulative voting **favors small shareholders**.
- Preferred: fixed dividend, priority over common, usually **no vote**, **rate-sensitive**.
- Rights = short-term, below market. Warrants = long-term, above market.
- ADR = foreign stock in US; **currency risk**, dividends in dollars.

**Debt — the see-saw**
- **Rates ↑ → bond prices ↓.** Longest maturity + lowest coupon = most sensitive.
- Discount bond yields: Nominal < Current < YTM < YTC. Premium: reversed (YTC lowest = worst).
- 1 bond point = **$10**. Quoted as % of $1,000 par.
- Treasuries: state-tax-free. **GNMA** = only agency with full faith & credit.
- Munis: **federal tax-exempt** → for high brackets; **GO** (taxing power, voter approval) vs.
  **revenue** (project income, riskier). Never put a muni in an IRA.
- Investment grade ≥ **BBB-/Baa3**. Commercial paper ≤ **270 days**, exempt.

**Pooled products**
- Open-end (mutual fund): unlimited shares, redeemed at **NAV** (forward pricing), priced daily.
- Closed-end: fixed shares, trades on exchange at premium/discount to NAV.
- Share classes: A = front load + breakpoints; B = back-end CDSC; C = level load (worst long-term).
- ETFs trade intraday; **leveraged/inverse reset daily → not for long holds**.
- REIT distributes **≥90%** of income. UIT = unmanaged, fixed.

**Options — Call Up, Put Down (what the buyer wants)**
- Long call/put max loss = premium. **Short (uncovered) call = unlimited loss.**
- Covered call = income + capped upside. Protective put = insurance.
- Contract = **100 shares**. ODD delivered at/before approval. OCC clears.

**Accounts & rules**
- Reg T initial **50%**; maintenance **25% long / 30% short**; **$2,000** to open margin.
- JTWROS → survivor. TIC → estate.
- Discretion = **A**ction/**A**sset/**A**mount (not price/time). Needs written authority.
- **Reg BI**: best interest; obligations = Disclosure, Care, Conflict, Compliance. **Form CRS**
  to retail.
- Settlement **T+1** (since 2024).

**Prohibited & AML**
- Churning, front-running, insider trading (penalty **3×**), selling away, freeriding (90-day
  freeze), guaranteeing against loss.
- **CTR** > $10,000 cash; **SAR** ≥ $5,000 suspicious (**don't tip off**). Placement → Layering
  → Integration. Screen **OFAC SDN**.
- Gifts capped **$100**/person/yr.

**Laws & regulators**
- **1933** = primary/new issues/prospectus. **1934** = secondary + created **SEC**.
- FINRA = SRO (exams, BrokerCheck). MSRB writes muni rules (doesn't enforce). SIPC = **$500k/
  $250k cash** if firm fails.
- **U4** onboard, **U5** terminate (within **30 days**). Statutory DQ look-back **10 years**.
- Communications: **>25 retail in 30 days = retail communication** (principal pre-approval).

## Practice question bank

> Answers and explanations follow each block. Cover them, answer, then check.

### Block A — Capital markets & economics (1–10)

1. An investor buys 100 shares of XYZ on the NYSE from another investor. The proceeds go to:
   A) XYZ Corporation  B) the selling investor  C) the SEC  D) FINRA

2. A firm trades a security out of its own inventory and charges a markup. It is acting as a:
   A) broker  B) agent  C) dealer (principal)  D) investment adviser

3. To stimulate a slowing economy, the FOMC would most likely:
   A) sell securities in the open market  B) raise the reserve requirement
   C) buy securities in the open market  D) raise the discount rate

4. Which is a leading economic indicator?
   A) unemployment rate  B) corporate profits  C) building permits  D) CPI

5. A recession is conventionally defined as:
   A) one quarter of falling GDP  B) two consecutive quarters of falling GDP
   C) rising unemployment for a year  D) a 20% stock-market decline

6. The interest rate banks charge each other for overnight loans is the:
   A) discount rate  B) prime rate  C) federal funds rate  D) call rate

7. A strong US dollar generally:
   A) helps US exporters  B) makes imports more expensive
   C) makes US goods cheaper abroad  D) makes imports cheaper for US buyers

8. Securities of unlisted companies that trade through a dealer network trade in the:
   A) first market  B) OTC market  C) fourth market  D) primary market

9. The market where an issuer first sells securities to raise capital is the:
   A) secondary market  B) third market  C) primary market  D) fourth market

10. A customer wants to buy a listed stock; the dealer fills it from inventory off-exchange.
    This is the:
    A) third market  B) fourth market  C) dark pool  D) primary market

**Answers A:** 1-B (secondary = investor-to-investor); 2-C (own inventory + markup = principal/
dealer); 3-C (buying adds reserves = easing); 4-C (permits predict activity); 5-B; 6-C; 7-D
(strong dollar buys more, so imports cheaper / exports pricier); 8-B; 9-C; 10-A (listed traded
OTC = third market).

## Scoring guide

- **Count correct ÷ 78.**
- **≥ 85%** — exam-ready; review only your missed topics.
- **70–84%** — passing-ish but thin; re-drill the weak blocks against the
  [main guide](01-sie-study-guide.md).
- **< 70%** — re-read the corresponding section before drilling again. Do not schedule the exam
  until practice scores clear 85% consistently.

> **Why drill past 70%.** The real exam pulls from a larger pool with fresh wording; a practice
> score has variance. Building to 85% gives you the margin to survive a few surprises and still
> clear the 70% line.
