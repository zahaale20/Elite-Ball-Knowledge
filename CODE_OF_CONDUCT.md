# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

This is a learning library built by many hands. We want it to be a place where anyone
— regardless of background, experience level, or where they are in their career — can
contribute ideas, ask "naive" questions, and be treated with respect.

## Expected behavior

- Be welcoming and patient, especially with newcomers and first-time contributors.
- Critique ideas and content, not people. Keep reviews specific and kind.
- Assume good faith. Ask clarifying questions before assuming the worst.
- Give credit. Acknowledge the work and ideas of others.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, insults, or discriminatory language of any kind.
- Personal or political attacks.
- Publishing others' private information without permission.
- Posting secrets, classified, or export-controlled material (see the repository's
  contribution rules — keep everything at an open, educational level).

## Scope

This applies to all repository spaces — issues, pull requests, discussions, and commit
history — and to public spaces when an individual is representing the project.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove contributions that violate this code, and may
block repeat offenders. To report a concern, open a private channel with a maintainer
(e.g. via the repository's contact/discussions link) rather than a public issue.
